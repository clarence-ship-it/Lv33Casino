Important Notice

Gambling can be addictive. Please play responsibly and within your means. If you feel that your gaming is becoming problematic, seek help immediately.

Self-Assessment

Ask yourself these important questions to evaluate your gaming habits:

  • Do you gamble more than you can afford to lose?
  • Do you need to gamble with increasing amounts of money to get the same thrill?
  • Have you tried to cut down or stop gambling but been unable to do so?
  • Do you feel restless or irritable when trying to cut down on gambling?
  • Do you gamble to escape from problems or relieve anxiety or depression?
  • Have you lied to family members or others to conceal your gambling activities?
  • Have you committed illegal acts to finance your gambling?
  • Have you risked or lost important relationships, jobs, or opportunities because of gambling?

If you answered "yes" to several of these questions, you may have a gambling problem and should consider seeking help.

Getting Help

If you or someone you know is struggling with gambling addiction, help is available:

Professional Organizations

  • Gamblers Anonymous: Free support groups worldwide
  • National Problem Gambling Helpline: 1-800-522-4700
  • GamCare: UK-based support and counseling
  • Gambling Therapy: Global online support community
  • BeGambleAware: Information and support resources

Warning Signs of Problem Gambling

  • Spending more money than intended
  • Neglecting responsibilities to gamble
  • Borrowing money to gamble
  • Feeling anxious or depressed about gambling
  • Lying about gambling activities
  • Chasing losses with bigger bets
